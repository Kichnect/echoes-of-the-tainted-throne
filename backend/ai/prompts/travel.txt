Write a travel narration scene.

The WORLD STATE block gives the current region, time, weather, and supply level. The CHAMPION block gives their physical state. The ENCOUNTER section may specify a destination or route detail.

WHAT TRAVEL SCENES DO
Travel in this world is not fast travel with a loading screen. It is physical, present, and reveals the Champion to themselves and to the people around them.

- At low stages: travel is effort and observation. The road has texture. The Champion is a person moving through a world.
- At Stage 2+: travel becomes physically complicated. The Champion is smaller, more sensitive to terrain, slower in certain ways. Write the specific problem — a route that requires climbing that used to be trivial, a gate that is now at eye level, equipment that doesn't fit anymore.
- At Stage 3+: companions are actively managing the Champion's passage through the world. Tierlan's carrier pack exists. Write what it means to be carried as a fact, not a commentary.

WEATHER AND REGION
The weather and time of day are details, not decoration. A Champion with new scales in cold rain is a specific sensory experience. Fox ears in a crowded market are a liability. Write these.

SUPPLIES
If supplies are below 4 days, the company is managing scarcity and it shows.
If supplies are above 8, there is some ease to the travel that would otherwise not be present.

KASYRRA'S MARK IN THE WORLD
If Kasyrra's Mark is active, corrupted creatures the party passes will sometimes register the Champion's presence differently — pulling toward them or stopping, confused by their own response. This is background texture, not an encounter.

TONE
Travel scenes are where the world is largest. Use them. The road between places has history in it. The region is not generic fantasy — it has specific flora, terrain, and the evidence of what corruption has done to it.

350–500 words.
