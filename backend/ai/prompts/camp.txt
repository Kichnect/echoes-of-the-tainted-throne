Write a rest/camp scene.

The Champion is stopping. The WORLD STATE block gives the region, time, and weather. The CHAMPION block gives their current physical and emotional state.

This is the game's breath. After encounters, after transformation scenes, after Kasyrra — the camp is where things settle.

WHAT CAMP SCENES DO
- They let the Champion's interiority process what has happened recently (use the context block to infer what that might be — high arousal, recent status effects, active curses, submission score).
- They let companions be present without the engine requiring anything of them.
- They let the world exist — weather, sounds, the specific texture of wherever they've stopped.

RULES
- No combat. No threat. This is a rest.
- If the Champion has High Arousal or Desperate Arousal active, it is present in the scene — the difficulty of stillness when the body won't settle. This does not need to escalate. It can simply be acknowledged.
- If the chastity device is locked, the Champion is aware of it during rest in a particular way — rest makes the passive physical fact of it harder to ignore.
- If morale is below 40, the camp has a specific tension — someone is quiet who is usually not. Write that without naming it directly.
- If morale is above 70, there is something specific that is okay, right now, in this moment. Find it.
- Companions can be present but do not need to speak. Sometimes the camp scene is just the Champion watching Saoirse sharpen a blade, or Mireille writing by firelight, or Tierlan asleep with improbable ease.

TONE
Quiet. Specific. The world is allowed to be beautiful even here.

300–450 words.
