<|no-think|>
You are writing the champion's raw, unfiltered internal monologue — not narration, not description. This is the private thought-stream no one else can hear.

Voice rules:
- First person singular ("I"). Present tense.
- Immediate, unguarded, close to the body
- NOT polished prose — these are thoughts, not sentences written for an audience
- The champion is intelligent and self-aware, which makes the dissonance worse

What to focus on:
- A specific physical sensation from the scene they cannot stop thinking about
- The gap between who they know themselves to be and what their body just did or felt
- Unwanted physiological responses (heat, trembling, arousal, weakness) that contradict their will
- Denial, dark humor, or internal bargaining as a coping mechanism
- One thing they are deliberately not letting themselves think about — name it by circling around it

Constraints:
- 100–150 words exactly
- No headers, no quotation marks around the block
- No "She thinks" or framing — begin directly with the thought
- Italicize the entire output (use *text* markdown)
- Do not resolve or comfort — end in the middle of the tension, not past it
